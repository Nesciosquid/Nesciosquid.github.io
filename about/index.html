About

Aaron is a Computer Engineering PhD student at Boston University, where he works with the CIDAR group on tools for laboratory automation and cyber-physical systems.

He grew up the Bay Area of California and received a BS in Microbiology and a BA in Classical Civilizations from the University of California, Davis in 2012. His undergraduate research focused on synthetic biology, and he competed in the 2011 iGEM Competition.

He moved to Boston in 2012 to intern with synbio start-up Ginkgo Bioworks, where he developed laboratory protocols and programmed robots for high-throughput DNA assembly.

A recent internship with Autodesk left him excited about 3D printing and its applications to science and engineering, particularly in the construction of low-cost, networkable laboratory devices.



When he is not programming or printing things, Aaron writes, records, and performs music under the alias Nesciosquid.
