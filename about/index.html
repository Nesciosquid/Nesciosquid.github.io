About Me

I’m currently a Computer Engineering PhD Student at Boston University, where I work with Prof. Thomas Little and the Multimedia Communications Lab to develop tools and techniques for indoor localization.

I like to make things like software, 3D printed devices, and music.

I teach a graduate-level Internet of Things course at BU, where our students create sensor networks, autonomous vehicles, and indoor localization systems from off-the-shelf consumer electronics. Course materials are available online through GitHub.

I grew up in the Bay Area and received a BS in Microbiology and a BA in Classical Civilizations from UC Davis in 2012. My previous work focused on synthetic biology and tools for laboratory automation.

I’m gay, I’m loud, and I want you to say hello!

You can contact me by email, Twitter, or by shouting my name really loud outside the SLURP Lab on the second floor of the BU Photonics Center. (Please don’t actually do that. People will think you’re weird.)
